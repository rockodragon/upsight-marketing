# Get started with UpSight

*Your first recorded customer call, in 10 minutes.*

> **Derived from:** `/70-PLG/onboarding/quickstart.md` (canonical source)
> Update there, then sync changes here.

---

This guide walks you through your first recorded customer call with UpSight. Have one on your calendar today? Even better — open this guide alongside it.

By the end, you'll have a transcribed call, evidence cards with the exact moments your customer said it, a draft follow-up email, and a contact profile that gets smarter with every conversation.

---

## Step 1 — Set up your account

Create an account at [getupsight.com](https://getupsight.com), or accept your invite if a teammate sent you one.

**First time, solo?** Take 2 minutes to add your account context: your company, what you sell, your ideal customer, and the key problems you solve. You set this once — it informs every prompt and every follow-up draft from then on.

**Joining a team?** Your admin already filled this in. Skip ahead to Step 3.

## Step 2 — Download the desktop agent

The desktop agent is what joins your meetings and runs In-Call Guidance.

[Download for macOS](#) · [Download for Windows](#)

Once installed, grant calendar and microphone permissions when prompted. You'll see a green "agent connected" indicator on the web app when it's ready.

> **[SCREENSHOT: desktop agent install + connected state]**

---

## Step 3 — Confirm your project's research goals

Open your project and look at the prompts.

These are different from your account context (Step 1). Account context describes who you are. Project prompts describe what you're trying to learn *from this round of calls*. They change with every project.

If your team has set them up already, just review them. If you're starting fresh, take 2 minutes to add 2–4 prompts. Examples:

- *"What slows down enterprise procurement?"*
- *"Why do customers churn after month 6?"*
- *"Which features come up most in renewal conversations?"*

Not sure where to start? Pick a template: **Sales Discovery · Customer Success Check-in · Stakeholder Interview · Custom**.

These prompts are your watch list. In-Call Guidance surfaces them during your call.

> **[SCREENSHOT: prompts editor + template picker]**

---

## Step 4 — Start your meeting. Hit record.

Open Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, or GoTo Meeting as you normally would.

In the desktop agent, click **Record**. UpSight joins the call, transcribes live, and identifies who's speaking.

You don't have to do anything else.

> **[SCREENSHOT: desktop agent record button + bot joined call view]**

---

## Step 5 — Watch the In-Call Guidance

While you're talking, your prompts surface in the sidebar — your own watch-fors, in your own words.

- **Skip** what's not useful.
- **Mark done** as items are addressed.
- **Add notes** anytime in the same panel.

Guidance is a suggestion, not a script. Your conversation, your call.

> **[SCREENSHOT: in-call guidance side panel with 2-3 prompts visible]**

---

## Step 6 — New signals every 30 seconds

As the call progresses, UpSight surfaces signals and tasks in real time:

- **Objections** ("They mentioned procurement is a bottleneck")
- **Buying signals** ("They asked about implementation timeline")
- **Pain points** ("They're spending 3 hours/week on this manually")
- **Feature requests** ("They asked if we integrate with Salesforce")

Edit them, mark them, or let them be. They're suggestions — not commitments.

> **[SCREENSHOT: signals/tasks list updating mid-call]**

---

## Step 7 — Stop the recording. Walk away.

End the meeting normally. UpSight processes the call in the background:

- Signals confirmed
- Tasks created
- Evidence cards built with timestamps
- Themes updated
- Contact profile refreshed

Processing usually takes 2–5 minutes. You don't have to wait.

> **[SCREENSHOT: processing state → completion notification]**

---

## Step 8 — The follow-up draft is waiting

When processing finishes, your follow-up email is drafted with:

- The next steps you actually agreed to
- Links to the exact evidence quotes from the call

Edit one line if you need to. Send.

> **[SCREENSHOT: follow-up draft view with evidence links]**

---

## Step 9 — Now their profile knows them

Click the contact you just spoke with.

Everything from the call is on their profile — quotes, signals, tasks, what's next. The next time you talk to them, UpSight already knows what they said today.

> **[SCREENSHOT: contact profile post-call with stacked evidence]**

## What to do next

**Do this for 3 calls in a week.**

That's when patterns start surfacing across conversations — common objections, recurring pain points, signals you'd otherwise miss — and the demo of UpSight stops being a demo.

## Troubleshooting

**The desktop agent didn't install.**
Check macOS "Privacy & Security" or Windows "User Access Control" permissions. If still stuck, [contact support](#).

**The bot didn't join my call.**
Confirm calendar integration is connected and the meeting platform is supported (Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, GoTo Meeting). For other platforms, use [Upload after the call](#) instead.

**The In-Call Guidance panel is too noisy.**
Click the gear icon → reduce density, or hide entirely for that call. Tell us what felt off — we tune this based on feedback.

**Processing is taking longer than 10 minutes.**
This is unusual. Check the dashboard processing-status indicator. If it's still spinning past 15 minutes, [contact support](#).

**I don't see a follow-up draft.**
Drafts generate after processing completes. If processing finished but the draft is missing, refresh the dashboard. If still missing, the email integration may need re-auth.
