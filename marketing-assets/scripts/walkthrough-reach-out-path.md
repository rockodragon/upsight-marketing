# Walkthrough — Reach Out Path (Survey → Responses → Patterns)

> **Maps to:** Steps-to-Wow **Amber Path** (user-success-journeys §6) · Activation Path: Survey Path
> **Primary personas served:** Product Lead, Founder, Post-Launch Founder
> **Aligned to:** Brand Brief v4.0 · Persona Flow Matrix v1.1
> **Target length:** 2:30–3:00 · ~360 words · moderate pace
> **Format:** 1920×1080 landscape · captions on by default · YouTube + in-app empty state + onboarding email

---

## Creative Brief

| | |
|---|---|
| **Job** | Unblock users who signed up but haven't added a conversation — give them a content-less path to first value. |
| **Audience** | Signed up users without recordings to upload. Product leads validating an idea, founders wanting to hear from quiet customers. |
| **One thing to believe** | "I can ask a good question and get patterns back — not a spreadsheet of 40 responses to re-read." |
| **Voice** | Clear, helpful, action-oriented. Contrarian against survey-fatigue clichés. |
| **Success metric** | % of viewers who create a survey + share it within 48h. |

---

## SCRIPT

### CHAPTER 1 — "Surveys usually suck. Here's the exception." (0:00–0:20)
**Visual:** Generic survey link ("Rate us 1–5") → crossed out with a soft strikethrough animation. Replaced with: a natural-language prompt box.
**On-screen:** **"A survey that actually tells you something."**
**VO:**
> "Most surveys give you averages and a spreadsheet nobody opens. This one gives you patterns — with the exact words your customers used. Three steps. Under three minutes. Let's go."

---

### CHAPTER 2 — STEP 1: Start from a research goal (0:20–0:50)
**Visual:** Screen recording. Inside a project, click "+ New Survey." A prompt appears: *"What do you want to learn?"* User types: *"What keeps our best customers from renewing on time?"* UpSight drafts 6 questions — mix of open-ended and targeted — with a suggested audience tag.
**On-screen callouts:**
- `// research goal in your words`
- `// open-ended where it matters`
**VO:**
> "Start with a goal — what you're actually trying to learn, in your own words. UpSight drafts the questions. Open-ended where the insight lives. Targeted where you need a number. Edit any of them. Or just send it."

---

### CHAPTER 3 — STEP 2: Share it (0:50–1:15)
**Visual:** Preview the survey — clean, mobile-ready, branded. Click "Share." Three paths appear: copy link · email list · embed in your app. User copies the link and pastes into a draft email to 10 customers.
**On-screen callouts:**
- `// no login required for respondents`
- `// works on mobile`
**VO:**
> "Share the link however you talk to your customers already. Email it. DM it. Drop it in Slack. Respondents don't need an account. They just answer."

---

### CHAPTER 4 — STEP 3: Watch patterns form, not just responses (1:15–2:00)
**Visual:** Jump forward — responses appearing live on the dashboard. Counter ticks: 3 · 5 · 8 · 12 responses. Then the page shifts: the raw response list fades to the background, and a **Themes** view fades to the foreground. Three themes surface with quote counts: *"Decision-maker turnover (4 mentions)"*, *"Pricing review window shifted to Q2 (3)"*, *"Integrations stalled with IT (3)."*
**On-screen callout:** `// synthesis while the survey is still running`
**VO:**
> "Here's the part that's different. As responses come in, UpSight doesn't just stack them up — it synthesizes them. Themes emerge in real time. You don't have to wait for the survey to close. You can act on patterns at five responses and sharpen them at fifty."

**Brand brief alignment:** §3.1 "Build conviction" / Interim analysis from persona-flow-matrix outcomes.

---

### CHAPTER 5 — THE RECEIPTS MOMENT (2:00–2:25)
**Visual:** Click the theme *"Decision-maker turnover."* Drawer opens with four verbatim quotes — each with the respondent's role, company size, and the exact question they answered.
**On-screen headline:** **"Four customers. Same signal. Their words."**
**VO:**
> "Click any pattern and see the exact words. Not a summary. Not 'the AI said.' The quotes, the roles, the context. When you walk into the room to make a call — you bring receipts."

---

### CHAPTER 6 — WHAT'S NEXT (2:25–2:45)
**Visual:** Assistant panel suggests two moves:
- *"Share this finding with your team"* (one-click link with evidence attached)
- *"Follow up with the 4 customers who mentioned this"* (drafts personalized outreach)

**VO:**
> "From here, two moves. Share the finding — the link keeps the evidence attached, so your team isn't arguing about averages. Or let an agent draft a follow-up to the four customers who raised this. Either way, you're closer to the answer than you were an hour ago."

---

### CHAPTER 7 — CTA (2:45–3:00)
**Visual:** UpSight logo. Amber CTA: *"Ask your customers one real question."*
**VO:**
> "Ask the question you've been meaning to ask. In an afternoon, you'll have patterns — not just responses."

**Super:** `getupsight.com/start`

## Production Notes

- **Use a real, anonymized survey.** The patterns need to feel like real patterns. No "Lorem ipsum" quotes.
- **Time-compress the response wait.** Jump-cut between 3 → 8 → 12 responses so it feels alive without dragging.
- **The "synthesis mid-survey" moment is the differentiator** vs. Typeform/SurveyMonkey. Slow this beat down 20% — let it land.
- **Captions on by default.**
