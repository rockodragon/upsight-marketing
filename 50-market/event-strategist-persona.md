## The Opportunity in One Sentence

Conference and event organizers can use UpSight to turn attendee conversations into premium audience intelligence — and sell that intelligence as a high-margin sponsorship tier that no competing event can offer.

---

## Why This Is Interesting

Most event organizers sell sponsors the same things: logo placement, booth space, badge scans, and session attendance counts. Every event at every price point offers this. It's commoditized and shrinking as sponsors demand ROI proof.

UpSight creates a new sponsorship product: **structured audience intelligence with receipts**.

Instead of "200 attendees visited your booth," the sponsor gets: "Here's what 200 attendees said about [sponsor's core problem space] — with themes, verbatim quotes, and the ability to click any insight and see exactly who said it."

That's a completely different product. And right now, no other event platform offers it.

## The Core Value Proposition

**Turn attendee conversations into sponsorship revenue.**

UpSight gives event organizers:
1. **Real-time audience capture** at events (kiosk mode — iPad stations where attendees share opinions on key topics)
2. **Structured synthesis** of what attendees said across demographics, roles, and interest areas
3. **Sponsor-ready intelligence reports** with themes, verbatim quotes, and source attribution
4. **A new premium sponsorship tier** — "Audience Intelligence Sponsor" — that no other event can offer

## How It Works

### At the Event (Kiosk Mode)

Set up 1–3 iPad kiosks in high-traffic areas (registration, networking lounges, sponsor booths). Each kiosk runs a short, structured conversation:

- AI voice agent greets each attendee (or text-based survey flow)
- 3–5 questions targeted to sponsor-relevant topics (e.g., "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [sponsor's problem area]?")
- Each session takes 2–4 minutes
- 50–200+ attendee conversations captured per event

### Post-Event (Synthesis)

UpSight synthesizes all kiosk conversations:
- Top themes across all attendees
- Breakdown by role, company size, or self-reported segment
- Verbatim quotes with source context
- Areas of strong consensus vs. disagreement
- Sponsor-specific filter: "What did attendees say specifically about [sponsor's domain]?"

### The Deliverable (Sponsor Report)

A structured intelligence report the sponsor actually wants to read:
- "67% of attendees at your target company size said [X] is their #1 priority"
- "Here are 8 verbatim quotes from decision-makers in your ICP about [relevant problem]"
- "3 emerging themes you didn't ask about — attendees kept bringing these up"
- Every insight clickable back to the specific conversation

This report:
- Validates that the right audience was in the room
- Gives the sponsor market research they couldn't get otherwise
- Provides content they can use internally and in marketing
- Makes the renewal conversation obvious: "Sponsor again, get the next year's intelligence"

## Activation Journey

### Entry
Organizer is preparing for their next event. They're working on the sponsor prospectus and realize they have no way to differentiate from competing events on ROI proof.

**Discovery channels:**
- LinkedIn content: "What if you could sell sponsors audience intelligence, not just impressions?"
- Direct outreach to event industry contacts
- Event industry communities (Event Leadership Institute, PCMA, Cvent user groups)
- Referral from consultants who run advisory board events

### First Value (Before the Event)
1. Organizer signs up, sets up their event in UpSight
2. Configures kiosk questions (UpSight suggests question templates by sponsor category)
3. Gets a preview of what the sponsor report will look like
4. Uses the preview to pitch the new "Audience Intelligence" sponsorship tier to 1–2 sponsors

**Activation hook**: The pitch deck for the new sponsorship tier — created from a sample UpSight report. The organizer is selling the intelligence package before the event even happens.

### Conviction (At and After Event)
1. Kiosk deployed at event — attendees engage (target: 50+ conversations per event)
2. UpSight synthesizes in near-real-time
3. Organizer shares the report with the intelligence sponsor within 48 hours of event close
4. Sponsor says "we didn't expect this much — we need to renew"

**Wow moment**: The sponsor report lands and the sponsor uses it in an internal meeting. They forward it to their marketing team. They ask for next year's event dates.

### Habit and Expansion
- Organizer builds the intelligence tier into every event's sponsor prospectus
- Annual recurring revenue from intelligence sponsors
- Adds pre-event surveys (what topics matter to your audience?) and post-event surveys (did we deliver?) as additional intelligence layers
- Eventually: competitor events see this and try to copy — but UpSight is embedded in the workflow

## Competitive Landscape

No direct competitor offers this exact value proposition:

| Tool | What It Does | Gap |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Cvent / Eventbrite** | Registration, logistics, badge scanning | No intelligence extraction, no synthesis |
| **Slido / Mentimeter** | Live polling and Q&A | Aggregate counts only, no qualitative synthesis |
| **Whova** | Event app with session feedback | Rating-based, no conversation intelligence |
| **Survey tools** (Typeform, etc.) | Post-event surveys | No real-time synthesis, no receipts |

**UpSight's edge**: The only tool that captures qualitative conversations, synthesizes themes with attribution, and delivers a report sponsors want to share internally.

## Connection to Broader Positioning

The Event Strategist is a **variant of the Consultant persona** — an operator who needs to deliver a client (the sponsor) with evidence-backed intelligence from conversations they facilitated. The same core value applies:

- Turn conversations into defensible intelligence
- Every insight grounded and attributable
- Deliver a report that justifies the relationship

The difference is the revenue model: instead of saving billable hours, the organizer is **creating a new revenue stream** from intelligence they couldn't sell before.
