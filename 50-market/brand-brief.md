## Executive Summary

UpSight is a **Customer Intelligence Platform** that helps growth-stage teams understand their customers and act on that understanding — without the manual grind.

We don't sell transcription. We don't sell research repositories. We sell what happens after the conversation: the follow-up that actually got sent, the pattern that surfaced before you asked, the decision that had evidence behind it, the deal that didn't go cold.

**Primary tagline**: Get your customers. Build conviction.

**Supporting message**: Know them. Serve them. Win them.

**One-sentence positioning**: UpSight captures every customer conversation, finds what matters, and puts AI agents to work — so your team spends less time digging and more time doing.

## 1. The Big Idea

### The Human Truth

**The best teams don't lack customer insight. They lose it — and then fail to act on it.**

Every day, smart people at growth-stage companies talk to customers. They listen. They take notes. They demo, they discovery-call, they follow up. And then most of what they learned disappears.

Into a Notion doc nobody reads. A Zoom recording nobody watches. A Slack thread that scrolls away. A sales call that never got logged. Someone's head when they leave the company.

**The tragedy isn't that you don't listen. It's that listening isn't enough.**

Because what good is insight if you can't find it when it matters? What good is a great discovery call if the follow-up is generic? What good is talking to customers if your product team still debates what they actually want — and your sales team is still flying blind on the next call?

### The Pain (In Their Words)

> "I know what's going on with our customers. My team doesn't."

That's the real problem. Not a lack of data. Not a lack of effort. A gap between what's known about customers and what the whole team can *find, act on, and build from*.

### The Aspiration

**What if every interaction with a customer made your whole team smarter — automatically?**

Not "I'll synthesize the notes later." Not "let me find that recording." Not "I thought sales already knew that."

Every call understood. Every pattern surfaced. Every follow-up ready. Every decision grounded in what customers actually said.

That's not a feature. That's a different way of running a company.

### The Promise

**Get your customers. Build conviction.**

UpSight captures what customers tell you, surfaces what matters, and puts agents to work — so you stop digging and start doing.

## 4. Category

### 4.1 Category Definition

**Customer Intelligence Platform**

Not a CRM. Not a research repository. Not a call recorder. Not an AI notetaker.

A platform that understands your customers from every conversation, then puts agents to work — so your team spends less time finding and more time doing.

### 4.2 What This Is Not

| Not This | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **Not a CRM** | CRM tracks pipeline and activity. UpSight extracts understanding and drives action from conversations. CRM features exist in the product but are not the value proposition. |
| **Not a research tool** | Research tools optimize for deep study. UpSight optimizes for fast, grounded decisions and daily workflow across sales, product, and CS. |
| **Not a notetaker** | Notetakers transcribe. UpSight understands, synthesizes, and acts. |
| **Not AI-only** | Human+AI hybrid. You choose voice agent, guided interview, upload, or live record. Agents assist; you lead. |

### 4.3 YC Validation: "Cursor for Product Managers"

Y Combinator's Spring 2026 Request for Startups lists **"Cursor for Product Managers"** as the #1 opportunity. Their framing:

> "The most important part is figuring out what to build in the first place."

**This is UpSight's pipeline.** Customer voice → grounded evidence → themes & insights → action.

Key point: ChatPRD helps you write the PRD. Cursor helps you write the code. UpSight helps you know if the PRD is worth writing — and then acts on what you learned.

### 6.2 Head-to-Head

| Competitor | Their Game | Our Angle | Threat Level |
|------------|------------|-----------|-------------|
| **VoicePanel** | AI-only voice research (YC W24) | Human+AI hybrid, multi-source, evidence trails, action connection | **Highest** |
| **Dovetail** | Research repository for UX teams ($69M raised) | For teams *without* dedicated researchers. Faster, action-first, agents do the follow-through. | **High** |
| **Gong** | Sales call coaching ($300M+ ARR) | For product + CS + founders, not just sales. Evidence for decisions and daily workflow. | **High** (different market) |
| **Day.AI** | "Cursor of CRM" — meeting assistant + context graph (Sequoia, $24M) | Evidence extraction, themes, structured analysis — they capture context, we extract understanding | **Medium-High** (converging) |
| **Looppanel** | Interview analysis ($27/mo) | End-to-end: capture, synthesize, act. Agents handle the follow-through. | **Medium** |
| **Notion + AI** | Docs with AI bolted on | Purpose-built conversation intelligence. Not a general-purpose tool. | **Low** |

### 6.3 Our Moat

**Hard to copy:**
- **Evidence graph**: The connection layer between insight and source across all conversation types
- **Multi-source synthesis**: Unified model across calls, surveys, tickets, notes, transcripts
- **Action pipeline**: Agents that go from insight to follow-up to task — not just a report
- **Research lenses**: Structured frameworks for analysis (not generic tagging)
- **Cross-role utility**: Serves product, sales, CS, and founders from one platform

**Easy to copy (not our moat):**
- Transcription (commodity)
- AI summaries (everyone has GPT)
- Pretty dashboards (design is table stakes)

## 8. Brand Voice

### 8.1 Brand Archetype

**The Trusted Operator**

Not the flashy visionary. Not the aggressive disruptor. The calm, experienced builder who's been in the room when decisions got hard and knows there's a better way.

We've shipped products. We've run the discovery calls. We've seen what happens when teams build on gut feel. We built UpSight because we lived this problem.

We're the person you want in the room when it matters. And now we put that experience to work for you.

### 8.2 Voice Principles

| Principle | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Direct** | Say it plainly. No jargon. | "See what customers said" not "Leverage voice-of-customer insights" |
| **Confident** | We've seen this work. No hedging. | "You'll have the follow-up ready before you close the laptop" not "AI may help you draft..." |
| **Action-first** | Lead with what gets done, not what gets analyzed. | "Follow-up drafted automatically" not "Insights surfaced for review" |
| **Grounded** | AI hype is everywhere. We're not. | "Grounded in what your customers actually said" not "Revolutionary AI-powered intelligence" |
| **Useful** | Help, don't lecture. | "Here's how to get started" not "Best practices for customer centricity" |

### 8.3 We Say / We Don't Say

| We Say | We Don't Say |
|--------|--------------|
| "Know your customers" | "Leverage voice-of-customer" |
| "Agents that act" | "AI-powered [anything]" |
| "Grounded in what they said" | "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" |
| "Follow-up writes itself" | "Drive alignment" |
| "Build the right thing" | "10x your customer understanding" |
| "See the source" | "Unlock the power of" |
| "Build conviction" | "Actionable intelligence" |
| "Every conversation, understood" | "Capture and analyze" |

**On "agents"**: Use positively and specifically. "Agents" is acceptable and encouraged when describing concrete actions the product takes on your behalf (drafting follow-ups, generating pre-call briefs, surfacing patterns, creating tasks). Avoid generic "AI agents" — be specific about what they do.
