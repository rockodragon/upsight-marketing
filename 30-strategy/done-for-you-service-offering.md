# Done-For-You Service Offering — "UpSight + a human in your corner"

> **Status:** New (2026-06-07) · proposal to validate this week
> **Thesis:** UpSight becomes a **platform with a done-for-you agency add-on.** The software gives a non-technical operator capability; the service gives them a human who helps them actually steer it. Custom pricing.
> **Related:** `positioning-brief.md` (2026-06-07 refresh), `pricing-strategy.md` (§9 + decision block), `../20-research/market-intel/Competitors/instagram-ad-wave-agentic-crm-2026-06.md` (the SalesAi "done-for-you" datapoint).

## Why this exists (the honest pitch)

We've got better software. But the honest truth is: **AI still won't do it *for* you.** A small, non-technical operator can't just be handed an agent and told "go." They need help steering it, getting the most out of advanced systems, and figuring out what's actually right for their business.

That help is a **service**, and it's worth paying for. So instead of pretending the software is magic, we say the true thing — and sell the steering as a premium layer. UpSight is the platform; the agency is the human in your corner.

This is also a competitive wedge. The agentic-CRM wave (Chirp, Reevo, Attention, Dispatch) sells "it just does it." One competitor, **SalesAi**, already sells fully done-for-you ("we build it, we run it, you book the jobs"). That validates demand — but it's pure outsourcing. Our bet is **empowerment with a safety net**: we do it *with* you and bring you "into the age," so you grow capable, not dependent.

## Who it's for

- Small operators / founders (5–50) who are **not technically savvy**, have no RevOps/ops hire, and won't self-serve their way to value.
- Service-business owners (home services, agencies, nonprofits, local B2B) who'd rather pay for an outcome than learn another tool.
- Buyers who say, in a sales call, *"this looks great, but I don't have time to set it up / I don't know what I'm doing."* (That sentence is the trigger to offer the service.)

## What's included (proposed)

**Setup / onboarding package (one-time):**
- Import their world (contacts, deals, conversations) and get the structured system of record stood up.
- Configure the rocks/moves model to their actual business + goals.
- Train Uppy on their voice, customers, and priorities; wire the agents that matter for them.
- A live "first wins" session — leave them with cracked rocks, not a blank app.

**Fractional AI operator (monthly retainer):**
- A human who reviews what Uppy surfaced, steers the agents, and keeps the system honest.
- Periodic working sessions: what are this month's rocks, what should the agents run, what's slipping.
- The accountability + judgment layer AI can't provide alone.

## Pricing model

**Custom / scoped per engagement** — do *not* put a number on the site for this tier; put "Talk to us."
- Rough shape to validate: **setup package** (one-time) + **monthly retainer** on top of a software seat.
- Anchor against what they'd pay a fractional exec / consultant / agency, not against the software price.
- Keep the self-serve software pricing transparent and low (Free / Starter / Team); the agency is the high-touch upsell, not a wall in front of the product.

## Positioning vs. pure done-for-you (SalesAi)

| | SalesAi (pure outsourcing) | UpSight + agency |
|---|---|---|
| Model | "We run it, you book jobs." | "We run it *with* you; you grow capable." |
| Software | Hidden / theirs | Yours, transparent, you keep it |
| End state | Dependent on the service | Empowered operator (+ optional ongoing help) |
| Risk | Churn when they question the bill | Stickier — they own the asset + relationship |

## Open questions (validate this week)

1. **Do small operators want "do it for me" or "make me capable"?** Likely both segments exist — which is the bigger near-term wallet? (Ask in the 5 pricing conversations.)
2. **Who delivers the service early?** Rick / founder-led at first (also the best learning loop), then a playbook → contractors. Capacity is the constraint; price accordingly.
3. **Setup-only vs. ongoing retainer** — which converts? Many SMBs will pay for setup but balk at a retainer; others want the ongoing hand. Offer both, watch the mix.
4. **Does the agency cannibalize or pull self-serve?** Hypothesis: it *pulls* — it de-risks the buy for the nervous non-technical buyer who'd otherwise bounce.
5. **Naming:** "Done-for-you," "Concierge," "Fractional AI operator," "UpSight Studio"? (Decide alongside the site update.)
