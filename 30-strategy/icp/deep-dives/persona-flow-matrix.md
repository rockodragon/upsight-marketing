## Company Size: 3–50 People

Teams small enough that one person wears multiple hats. Big enough that insights need to be shared, not just remembered. Below 3, the product solves a nice-to-have. Above 50, they have dedicated teams and budgets for Dovetail + Gong + a researcher.

**Common traits across all personas:**
- Talk to customers weekly (not quarterly research projects)
- Want to be evidence-driven but lack infrastructure
- Resource-constrained — can't afford 3 separate tools
- No formal UX/research training (or very little)
- Need insights to drive action, not reports

## The 5 Personas

### 1. Consultant / Fractional Exec *(Beachhead)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Solo consultant, fractional CRO/CPO/CTO, boutique firm principal. Runs 5–15 stakeholder interviews per engagement. Bills $150–300/hr. Buys tools alone, no procurement. |
| **Reality** | Drowning in stakeholder interviews, spending 2–3 days synthesizing before they can deliver clarity. Every hour of synthesis is non-billable. |
| **Fear** | Recommendations that get challenged. SOWs built on gut feeling. Presenting without receipts. |
| **Dream** | Walk into every client meeting with undeniable evidence. Same-day synthesis. Defensible recommendations. |
| **Outcome** | Build conviction in their SOW and implementation plan |
| **Message** | *"From stakeholder interviews to undeniable recommendations."* |
| **ROI proof** | 8–12 hours saved per project @ $200/hr = $1,600–2,400 saved per engagement |
| **Activation hook** | Upload first client recording → same-day synthesis with receipts |

#### Variant: Event Strategist

Conference and event organizers who need attendee and sponsor intelligence. Uses kiosk + surveys at events to capture real-time audience signal, then packages it as sponsorship intelligence.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Event planners, conference organizers, community managers running 2–10 events/year with sponsors. |
| **Reality** | Sells sponsorships on impression counts and attendee numbers. Sponsors want proof of ROI but events can only offer badge scans and booth traffic. |
| **Fear** | Sponsors don't renew because they can't see the value. Competing events offer the same generic metrics. |
| **Dream** | "Here's what 200 attendees said about [sponsor's problem space]" as a sponsorship upsell. Evidence-backed audience intelligence that no other event can offer. |
| **Outcome** | Higher sponsorship revenue through differentiated audience intelligence |
| **Message** | *"Turn attendee conversations into sponsorship revenue."* |
| **ROI proof** | One retained $50K sponsor pays for the tool for years. Audience intelligence as a premium sponsorship tier. |
| **Activation hook** | Set up kiosk at event → capture 50+ attendee conversations → package themes into sponsor report with receipts |
| **Key feature** | Kiosk (real-time capture) + surveys (pre/post event) + synthesis (sponsor-ready report) |

### 2. Founder *(Fast-Follow)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | CEO/founder at 3–50 person company. Post-launch, seed to Series A. Talks to customers weekly — demos, support, discovery calls. Buys fast, no procurement. |
| **Reality** | Daily job is serve customers + close deals + decide what's next. Juggles 20+ relationships across prospects, customers, and churned users. Context lives in their head. When they're sick for a week, nothing moves. |
| **Fear** | Dropping the ball on a hot lead. Forgetting what a customer told them last month. Losing a deal because the follow-up was too slow or too generic. |
| **Dream** | Every customer relationship is tracked, every follow-up is timely, every conversation makes the next one smarter. Nothing falls through the cracks. |
| **Outcome** | Never drop the ball — serve better, sell more, decide faster |
| **Message** | *"Know every customer. Never drop the ball."* |
| **ROI proof** | One saved deal per quarter covers the tool. One avoided churn saves 6 months of re-acquisition. |
| **Activation hook** | Upload recent customer calls → see who needs follow-up → get a draft email ready to send |

### 3. Post-Launch Founder *(High Urgency)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Technical or product-focused founder. Shipped but growth stalled. <$10K MRR, <18 months runway. Has 10–30 existing customer conversations they haven't analyzed. Has a contact list (customers, prospects, churned users). |
| **Reality** | Sitting on a pile of customer conversations they haven't had time to synthesize. Growth is flat. Board wants evidence, not opinions. Needs to figure out: are we selling wrong, building wrong, or talking to the wrong people? |
| **Fear** | Burning remaining runway on the wrong bet. Missing the buying signal that's already in their data. Finding out too late that 8 customers told them the same thing and they didn't hear it. |
| **Dream** | Upload everything, get clarity in hours. Know which customers are worth doubling down on. Walk into the board meeting with evidence, not a hunch. Then shift to ongoing mode — never lose signal again. |
| **Outcome** | Find the signal in existing data, then become a daily user |
| **Message** | *"Your customers already told you what's working. Hear them."* |
| **ROI proof** | Clarity on direction in 2 hours vs. 2 weeks of manual synthesis. One correct pivot decision = survival. |
| **Activation hook** | Bulk upload recordings + contact list in parallel → cross-conversation synthesis within hours |
| **Note** | This is an **activation path**, not a permanent persona. After initial synthesis, Post-Launch Founders become regular Founders using UpSight daily. |

### 4. Product Lead *(Secondary)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | PM, head of product, product designer at 10–50 person company. No dedicated UX researcher. Does customer research themselves alongside building. May partner with external consultants. |
| **Reality** | Owns customer insight but has no system. Research lives in Notion docs nobody reads. Builds features, then discovers they missed the mark. |
| **Fear** | Research that dies in a slide deck. Sprint planning based on opinions, not evidence. |
| **Dream** | Evidence that actually changes what gets built. Research that survives the journey from interview to sprint ticket. |
| **Outcome** | Build conviction in the roadmap |
| **Message** | *"Research that ships."* |
| **ROI proof** | One avoided wrong feature = 2–4 weeks of engineering saved |
| **Activation hook** | Upload user interviews → themes linked to backlog → share evidence before sprint planning |

### 5. Sales / BD Lead *(Revenue Driver)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who** | AE, SDR, BD lead, or founder-doing-sales at 3–50 person company. Runs 5–20 prospect calls per week. Uses a CRM but doesn't get intelligence from it. Can't afford Gong ($1,200+/seat/year). |
| **Reality** | Takes call notes by hand. Forgets what prospects said last time. Doesn't prep well enough for follow-ups. Pipeline management is a spreadsheet or gut feel. No cross-deal pattern visibility. |
| **Fear** | Losing deals they should've won because they didn't follow up right, didn't remember what the prospect cared about, or didn't catch the buying signal. |
| **Dream** | Walk into every call knowing exactly what to say. After every call, follow-up writes itself. Pipeline tells them where deals actually stand, not where the CRM says they stand. |
| **Outcome** | Close more deals by never dropping context |
| **Message** | *"Never walk into a call unprepared. Never let a deal go cold."* |
| **ROI proof** | One saved deal per quarter pays for the tool 10×. Gong costs $100+/user/mo — UpSight gives 80% of the value at 20% of the cost. |
| **Activation hook** | Record first sales call → AI extracts buying signals, objections, next steps → follow-up email drafted automatically |

## Intent Matrix (Modes × Personas)

These are **modes any persona uses** — not personas themselves. The AI shifts behavior based on the user's intent with a specific contact.

| Intent | What It Means | Consultant | Founder | Post-Launch | Product Lead | Sales |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Discover** | First contact, learn who they are | First stakeholder call | First customer conversation | First pass through existing data | First user interview | ★ Discovery/demo call |
| **Qualify** | Should I invest more time? | Is this client a good fit? | Should we build this? | ★ Pivot or persevere? | Prioritize this user's feedback? | ★ Is this a real deal? |
| **Deepen** | Build the relationship, learn more | ★ Multi-stakeholder mapping | ★ Ongoing customer relationship | Deepen with champions | Follow-up interview | ★ Multi-threading the account |
| **Close** | Drive to a decision or outcome | Deliver SOW, get sign-off | ★ Ship the right feature | ★ Present to board | Get sprint commitment | ★ Close the deal |
| **Re-engage** | They've gone cold | Follow up between projects | Re-engage cold customer | ★ Win back churned users | Follow up on survey | ★ Revive stalled deal |

★ = primary intent for this persona

**Key insight for Sales**: `Close` and `Qualify` are the stars. Gong optimizes for coaching *after* calls. We optimize for **preparation before** and **action after** — the parts that actually close deals.

## What the UI Must Deliver (Persona × Screen)

How the three tabs serve each persona:

### Priorities Tab

| Persona | What They See |
|---|---|
| **Consultant** | Client deliverables due, stakeholder follow-ups, mid-project synthesis tasks |
| **Founder** | Who to follow up with, team debates needing evidence, product decisions pending |
| **Post-Launch** | Who to re-engage, hypotheses to validate, board prep tasks |
| **Product Lead** | Research tasks, surveys to send, sprint evidence to share |
| **Sales** | ★ Follow-up list by urgency, deals at risk, pipeline action items, calls today |

### Contacts Tab

| Persona | What They See |
|---|---|
| **Consultant** | Client stakeholder map, ICP scores, engagement signals |
| **Founder** | Customer list with health scores, who's active, who's cold |
| **Post-Launch** | All contacts with signal strength, segment by fit |
| **Product Lead** | Research participants, persona assignments, coverage gaps |
| **Sales** | ★ Prospect list with deal stage, buying signals, last touch, ICP score |

### Insights Tab

| Persona | What They See |
|---|---|
| **Consultant** | Cross-stakeholder themes, agreement/disagreement map, client-ready report |
| **Founder** | Top themes, what customers are asking for, evidence snapshots to share |
| **Post-Launch** | Cross-conversation patterns, pivot signals, validation/invalidation evidence |
| **Product Lead** | Themes linked to backlog, survey results, research gaps |
| **Sales** | ★ Common objections, winning messages, competitive mentions, deal patterns |

### Assistant Panel (Context-Aware)

| Trigger | What It Suggests |
|---|---|
| Calendar event in 2 hours | "Pre-call briefing for [Name] — here's what they said last time" |
| Call just uploaded | "Draft follow-up email" · "Connect on LinkedIn" · "Log buying signals" |
| Contact cold 10+ days | "Re-engage [Name]" · "Draft a check-in" |
| Survey has 5+ responses | "See interim patterns" · "Share early findings" |
| Monday morning | "Your week: [N] follow-ups, [N] calls, [N] pipeline actions" |
| Deal stage changed | "Sarah moved to negotiation — prep pricing 1-pager" |
| 3+ contacts mention same topic | "Emerging pattern: [theme] — share with team?" |
| New contact added | "Score ICP fit" · "What should we ask them first?" |
