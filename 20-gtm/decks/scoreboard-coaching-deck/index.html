SECTION 05 / TWO THEORIES
08 / 18
Section 05 · Pre-revenue vs Revenue

Same bones, different theories
of the business.

Pre-revenue and revenue templates measure different things — not the same things with different numbers.

Theory 1 · Pre-revenue
Learning velocity & commitment signals
Every qualified conversation, every scoped pilot, every signed LOI is the business. Magnitude is irrelevant — state changes are everything.
Theory 2 · Post-PMF
Recurring economics & sales efficiency
Once you cross PMF, the question shifts from "can we get someone to commit?" to "how predictable is the engine?" MRR, CAC payback, win rate, cycle time become real signals.
The inflection rule
Use pre-rev until $1 from 3 sources
The first $50 customer is a state change. The 50th is a magnitude. Don't try to use one board for both phases.

SECTION 07 / CONFIGURABILITY
12 / 18
A warning

Configurable Opinionated.
Blank canvases die in onboarding.

  • 5 templates, opinionated. Onboarding asks ONE question: "what does paying you look like?"
  • Per-metric overrides only: label, target, period, formula-from-catalog. No free-form SQL. Ever.
  • The catalog is the truth. The template is the opinion. The config is the customization. In that order.
  • If you can't ship a default, you don't have a product. You have a spreadsheet generator.
SECTION 08 / SHIP FIRST
13 / 18
Atomic habits compound — even in code

Don't ship the configurable scoreboard. Ship the one for you.

01
Pick one pipeline view. Kill the other. The duplicate is the symptom, not the bug.
02
Move scoreboard to the Today screen. North Star + 3 leading indicators + 3 actions.
03
Build phase 1: hard-coded pre-revenue B2B SaaS template. No selector. No settings.
04
Dogfood 2 weeks. Does it change what you do Monday morning? If not, fix it before generalizing.
05
Then templatize. Then snapshots + drill-down. Then gen-UI inline.

The atomic habit you're building isn't "look at metrics." It's the weekly review.

PUSHBACK / FIVE THINGS TO HEAR
15 / 18
Read these like a tough counselor wrote them — because one did
  • Don't put strategic metrics in the pipeline view. That's exactly why you ended up with two pipeline versions in the first place.
  • Pipeline is sales operations. Scoreboard is the operator's box score. Different altitudes, different surfaces, different rooms.
  • "Pipeline potential MRR" unweighted is theater. Weight it by stage probability or drop it. Vanity dressed as forecast is the worst kind of metric.
  • Targets that are red every week train you to ignore red lights. Set stretching but plausible. Then defend them.
  • Configurability is earned—by proving the opinionated version works first. Not by promising it does in the roadmap.
