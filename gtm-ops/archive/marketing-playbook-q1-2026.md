## Three Marketing Themes

### Theme 1: "From Conversations to Conviction to Traction"

**The CEO story.** Not just "understand your customers" — that's table stakes. The full arc: engage the right people, build evidence-backed conviction, then act on it and track traction. UpSight closes the loop from customer voice to shipped outcomes.

**Messaging:**
- "Stop debating. Start knowing. Start shipping."
- "Every insight links to action. Every action links to evidence."
- "The gap isn't listening — it's doing something about what you heard."

**Homepage tie-in:** Marcus (Founder & CEO) persona — "Build conviction to act"

**Target audience:** Founders, CEOs, product leaders at 5-50 person companies

**Content:**
- **Blog:** "Product-Market Fit Signal Tracker: What to Measure in Customer Conversations" (Keyword: `product-market fit signals`, 300-600/mo, Low difficulty)
- **Blog:** "How to Stop Opinion-Driven Roadmaps in Early-Stage Companies" (Cluster 5)
- **Remotion video (60-90s):** Split-screen — left: chaotic Slack debate about what to build. Right: UpSight evidence view where 4/5 customers name the same pain point. End frame: "The evidence made the decision obvious." Then show: insight → task created → shipped → customer validated.
- **Product shots:** (1) Insight card with linked evidence "receipts", (2) Task/action created from insight with source attribution, (3) Journey map showing evidence-backed decision points
- **LinkedIn founder posts:** Rick's personal account, 3x/week. "Here's what our customers actually told us this week and what we did about it." Eat your own cooking.

**Keywords:** `customer discovery tool`, `customer evidence platform`, `turn customer feedback into decisions`, `product-market fit signals`

---

### Theme 2: "Know Your Community" (Surveys + Audience Intelligence)

**The community leader story.** You run events, manage communities, advocate for developers or constituents. You need to hear from them — not guess. UpSight surveys let you engage your audience, capture voice at events, and surface what actually matters to the people you serve.

**Messaging:**
- "Your community is talking. Are you capturing it?"
- "From event conversations to community intelligence in minutes."
- "Don't assume what your community needs. Ask them. Then prove it."

**Target audience:** DevRel in tech, non-profit leaders, community organizers, event producers, advocacy orgs

**Why this audience:** Underserved by competitors (Dovetail/Looppanel target UX researchers). Community leaders have the same synthesis problem — lots of conversations, no structured evidence. Surveys are the entry wedge; in-person event capture is the differentiator.

**Content:**
- **Blog:** "How We Captured 30 Voices at International Women's Day (And What We Learned)" — use the real IWD event footage, quotes, and sound bites as a case study
- **Blog:** "The DevRel Research Stack: How to Actually Know What Your Developer Community Wants"
- **Remotion video (60-90s):** Show the IWD event flow — in-person interview → upload → AI extracts themes → community intelligence dashboard. Real footage intercut with product UI. End: "Every voice captured. Every pattern visible."
- **Remotion video (30s social cut):** Best 3 sound bites from IWD interviews with UpSight branding overlay. Optimized for LinkedIn/Twitter.
- **Product shots:** (1) Survey builder with community-oriented questions, (2) Response grid showing diverse participant voices, (3) Theme clustering from event interviews — patterns emerging across 30 conversations
- **Outreach:** DevRel Slack communities (DevRel Collective, Community Club), non-profit tech groups, Women in Tech networks, event organizer forums

**Keywords:** `community feedback tool`, `event feedback analysis`, `developer community survey`, `stakeholder interview analysis tool`

**Asset:** IWD event content — video clips, audio sound bites, interview transcripts. Use as proof-of-concept showcase.

### Theme 3: "Turn Interviews into Decisions, Not Decks"

**The practitioner story.** PMs, consultants, and UX researchers who do the interviews but struggle with synthesis. UpSight's core value prop: evidence traceability. Every insight shows its receipts.

**Messaging:**
- "From conversations to conviction — with receipts."
- "You did the interviews. Now prove what you learned."
- "Research that ships, not research that sits."

**Homepage tie-in:** Sarah (Product Lead) persona — "Ship what customers asked for"

**Target audience:** Product managers, UX researchers, consultants/fractional execs (beachhead)

**Content:**
- **Blog:** "How to Synthesize 10 Customer Interviews in 2 Hours (Not 2 Weeks)" (Keyword: `how to synthesize customer interviews`, 300-600/mo, Low difficulty)
- **Blog:** "Consultant Playbook: Turning Stakeholder Interviews into Defensible Recommendations" (Cluster 5)
- **Remotion video (60-90s):** The transformation: upload 5 recordings → AI extracts 47 evidence cards → themes auto-cluster → publish insight with source links. Emphasize: "every claim links to the exact quote, speaker, and timestamp."
- **Product shots:** (1) Evidence cards with highlighted quotes + speaker attribution, (2) Theme clustering view showing patterns emerging, (3) Side-by-side: old way (scattered notes, slides) vs. UpSight (searchable, linked evidence)
- **Comparison pages:** `/alternative-to-dovetail`, `/alternative-to-looppanel` (Keyword: `Dovetail alternative`, 500-1K/mo)

**Keywords:** `how to analyze customer interviews`, `customer interview analysis software`, `qualitative research software`, `Dovetail alternative`, `continuous discovery tool`
