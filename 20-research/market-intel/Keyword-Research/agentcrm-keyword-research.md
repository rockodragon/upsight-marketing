## 2. Content Angles (7 Blog Post Ideas)

### 1. "Why Your AI Agent Is Flying Blind Without Customer Context (And How MCP Fixes It)"
**Target**: AI-forward developers using Claude/Cursor
**Keywords**: "MCP server CRM", "customer data for AI agents"
**Angle**: Explains how MCP + UpSight gives agents memory of every customer interaction.

### 2. "Evidence-Based Selling: The Post-CRM Framework for Founder-Led Sales"
**Target**: Seed/Series A founders
**Keywords**: "evidence-based selling", "CRM for founder-led sales"
**Angle**: Positions "receipts" as the new unit of CRM — not contacts or deals, but verified quotes and moments. Category-creation piece.

### 3. "Gong vs. Day.ai vs. UpSight: Conversation Intelligence for Teams That Actually Build With AI"
**Target**: PMs and sales leaders evaluating tools
**Keywords**: "Gong alternative", "AI native CRM"
**Angle**: Honest comparison that positions UpSight as the builder-friendly option with API/MCP access.

### 4. "The Conversation Intelligence Stack for Agentic Workflows"
**Target**: Technical PMs and developers building AI agents
**Keywords**: "conversation intelligence for AI agents", "agentic CRM"
**Angle**: Shows architecture: recordings -> transcripts -> evidence -> themes -> MCP server -> agent actions.

### 5. "Stop Logging Calls, Start Collecting Receipts: How AI Turns Conversations Into Searchable Evidence"
**Target**: Sales leaders frustrated with Gong's passive recording model
**Keywords**: "conversation intelligence platform", "conversation receipts CRM"
**Angle**: Positions UpSight's "lenses" and "receipts" as active intelligence extraction.

### 6. "Building a Customer Intelligence API: What We Learned Shipping MCP for CRM Data"
**Target**: Developer audience
**Keywords**: "customer intelligence API", "MCP server CRM"
**Angle**: Technical deep-dive on exposing customer evidence via MCP. Very few companies are writing developer content about CRM + MCP.

### 7. "What Gong, Attio, and Day.ai Get Wrong About Customer Context"
**Target**: Evaluators comparing tools
**Keywords**: "Attio alternative", "AI CRM for startups"
**Angle**: Gong records but doesn't structure evidence; Attio structures data but ignores conversations; Day.ai captures context but doesn't expose it programmatically. UpSight does all three.

## 3. Competitor Content Gaps

### What Gong is NOT writing about:
- **Developer/API audience** — Gong's blog is 100% aimed at revenue leaders and sales managers. Zero content for builders, developers, or AI-agent use cases.
- **MCP / agentic integration** — Gong published one post about "context graphs" but has no content about making conversation data available to AI agents via standard protocols.
- **Small team / founder-led sales** — Gong's pricing ($15K-$65K implementation) and content both target enterprise. They've abandoned the startup segment entirely.
- **Evidence as a data primitive** — Gong talks about "insights" but never about verified, attributable, searchable evidence with provenance. The "receipts" concept is unclaimed.

### What Attio is NOT writing about:
- **Conversation intelligence** — Attio added call intelligence as a feature but publishes zero content about conversation analysis, meeting insights, or voice data.
- **AI agents consuming CRM data** — Attio supports MCP but has no content educating developers on how to build agents that use CRM context.
- **Unstructured-to-structured pipeline** — Attio's strength is structured data. They have no content about turning raw conversations into structured evidence.

### What Day.ai is NOT writing about:
- **Programmatic access / API-first** — Day.ai's pitch is "just talk to it." No SDK, no public API content, no developer docs. Alienates the AI-builder audience.
- **Multi-framework analysis (lenses)** — Day.ai captures context but doesn't let users apply structured analytical frameworks to conversations.
- **Transparency / evidence provenance** — Day.ai emphasizes AI-generated summaries but not verifiable attribution back to specific moments.

### The unclaimed territory UpSight should own:
1. **"Conversation intelligence for the agentic era"** — The intersection of conversation data + MCP + AI agent workflows. Nobody is writing here.
2. **"Receipts, not summaries"** — Verifiable, attributed evidence vs. black-box AI summaries. Trust-forward positioning.
3. **"Customer intelligence as infrastructure"** — API/MCP-first approach to making conversation insights available to any tool in the stack.
