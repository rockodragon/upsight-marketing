## Captured summary text (verbatim — second meeting: Cytodyme + Tanner Ash, CDMO)

> Pasted by Rick from Apollo's generated Summary. Kept verbatim as the reference specimen the teardown reacts to.

**Outcome**
DJ White from Cytodyne and T A discussed the potential for Cytodyne's solutions to address T A's organization's needs during their transition to a CDMO. T A expressed interest in a proof of concept and acknowledged the competitive pricing of Cytodyne's offerings compared to other solutions. They agreed on the importance of a strategic approach to present to higher management and planned to share materials with key stakeholders, including Ellen and Carrie, to facilitate discussions on software purchases. DJ White committed to sending a corporate deck and emphasized their willingness to work collaboratively to secure the business.

**Pricing**
In the conversation, DJ White discusses pricing with T A regarding their services. He mentions that the cost for all three sites would be approximately $150,000, stating, "it would probably be like 150k or something like that for all three sites to have access to it." T A acknowledges that this price is significantly lower than what competitors like Viva would offer, indicating that "there's no way Viva and some of those other companies are going to be that low for that many sites." DJ White further emphasizes their competitive pricing strategy by saying, "we want your business," and assures T A that they will beat the prices of other solutions available in the market.

**Next Steps**
DJ White — Send the corporate deck covering the solutions to T A for forwarding to Ellen and Carrie.
Tanner Ash — Set up a meeting with Carrie for early next week, preferably Monday, and check her availability. Forward the corporate deck to Ellen and Carrie once received. Discuss the AI implementation project with Carrie to gather insights and prepare for the meeting.

**Objections**
Tanner Ash
- DJ White recommended a fully integrated system for 150k for all three sites; T A was concerned that their organization is undergoing a transition to Rose, which has increased workload and complexity, making it difficult to focus on software purchases.
- DJ White recommended they could easily grow the contract; T A was concerned they need a clear strategy and proposal to present to higher management to gain buy-in.
- DJ White recommended sharing the corporate deck with Carrie; T A was concerned they have been excluded from discussions regarding software purchases, limiting their ability to advocate.
- DJ White recommended Cytodyne's pricing would be cheaper; T A was concerned about budget constraints and identifying key stakeholders for collaboration.
- DJ White recommended a proof of concept; T A was concerned this would need to be part of a broader strategy for future collaboration.

**Pain Points**
Tanner Ash
- Transitioning to Rose has increased workload and complexity.
- Strong need for AI integration as they transition to a CDMO to remain competitive.
- Existing tools (Smartsheet, Excel trackers) seen as inefficient vs. previously used Agile Place Plan View.
- Feels excluded from software-purchase discussions.
- Needs a clear strategy/proposal for higher management buy-in.
- Budget constraints; needs to identify key stakeholders.
- Interested in a proof of concept, but as part of a broader strategy.
