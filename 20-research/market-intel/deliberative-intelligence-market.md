# Deliberative Intelligence Market Assessment

Date: 2026-06-02
Status: working market thesis

## Executive Take

UpSight can credibly be described in product-architecture terms as a deliberative intelligence platform, but that is not yet the best commercial category to sell. The market category is still unproven: buyers do not yet wake up saying, "I need deliberative intelligence." They do wake up saying:

- "We cannot tell what customers actually agree on."
- "Sales, product, research, and leadership are arguing from different anecdotes."
- "Our research summaries do not change decisions."
- "We need to prove which segments support this direction and where disagreement remains."
- "AI summaries are useful, but nobody trusts them without evidence."

The most viable wedge is:

**Evidence-backed alignment for high-stakes product and strategy decisions.**

Customer discovery is the beachhead, but the larger commercial opportunity is trusted synthesis across messy human input: interviews, surveys, meetings, customer calls, advisory boards, employee conversations, and strategic planning sessions.

## Core Commercial Hypothesis

UpSight should not initially sell "deliberative democracy" or "collective intelligence." Those labels are accurate to the architecture but weak as near-term budget language.

The sellable problem is decision trust:

> UpSight shows where customers and stakeholders agree, where they disagree, which segments are driving each signal, and the evidence behind every recommendation.

That is concrete enough for product, research, strategy, and GTM leaders to buy.

## Best Initial ICP

### Primary ICP

**B2B product organizations with 200-3,000 employees, active customer research, noisy stakeholder input, and expensive roadmap or strategy decisions.**

These teams already collect substantial qualitative input but struggle to turn it into trusted, organization-wide decisions.

### Ideal Buyer

| Role | Why They Buy |
|---|---|
| VP Product / CPO | Needs roadmap confidence and cross-functional alignment |
| Head of Research / Research Ops | Needs evidence to influence decisions, not just summarize studies |
| Product Strategy / Product Ops | Needs to synthesize customer, sales, support, and market signals |
| Customer Insights / VOC lead | Needs a trusted cross-source customer evidence system |
| COO / Chief of Staff | Needs alignment across exec, customer, and team input |

### Champion

| Champion | What They Want |
|---|---|
| Research Ops | Less manual synthesis, stronger evidence traceability |
| Product Ops | Better prioritization inputs and stakeholder alignment |
| UX Research | A way to preserve nuance and prove impact |
| Founder / CEO | Fast signal from customer conversations without research bottlenecks |

### Buying Trigger Events

| Trigger | Why It Opens Budget |
|---|---|
| New product line or strategic repositioning | Need broad customer evidence fast |
| Roadmap conflict between sales, product, and founders | Need neutral evidence-backed alignment |
| Enterprise customer advisory board | Need structured synthesis and follow-up |
| Churn, win/loss, or ICP confusion | Need cross-source diagnosis |
| Research repository failing to influence decisions | Replacement or augmentation budget |
| AI mandate from leadership | Budget exists, but trust and governance concerns remain |

### Dovetail

Dovetail is the closest adjacent competitor on the product/research side. It already positions around customer intelligence and offers AI summaries, semantic search, AI clustering, dashboards, Slack/Teams querying, AI suggested actions, calendar import, CRM integrations, enterprise security, and custom pricing.

Implication: UpSight should not position as an "AI research repository." That framing walks directly into Dovetail's current market narrative.

UpSight needs to emphasize:

- agreement and disagreement mapping
- segment/persona-theme matrices
- confidence and unknowns
- decision recommendations
- source provenance for every claim
- cross-source evidence, not just research project organization

### Civic Engagement Platforms

Zencity and Go Vocal/CitizenLab validate that governments buy AI-assisted public input analysis and engagement platforms. Go Vocal says CitizenLab/Go Vocal has supported 1M+ community members and 500+ local governments and organizations. Zencity positions itself as a government-focused platform using AI-driven analytics to help leaders make informed, transparent decisions.

Implication: civic engagement is strategically aligned, but not the right first wedge unless there is a strong distribution partner or anchor customer. Procurement, political trust, accessibility, public transparency, and data governance requirements will slow sales.

## UpSight's Advantage

UpSight's strongest advantage is architectural, not just feature-level.

| Advantage | Why It Matters Commercially |
|---|---|
| Evidence-first data model | Buyers can audit claims and trust outputs |
| Multi-lens synthesis | Same input serves product, sales, research, strategy, and support |
| Agreement and disagreement potential | Most tools summarize; few preserve conflict well |
| Segment/persona matrices | Turns qualitative input into prioritization evidence |
| Source traceability | Reduces the "AI made it up" objection |
| Async survey + interview ingestion | Captures more than scheduled research interviews |
| Confidence and unknowns | Makes outputs decision-grade rather than decorative |
| Action linkage | Connects insight to task, CRM, roadmap, or stakeholder follow-up |

The strategic differentiator:

> UpSight does not just summarize feedback. It shows what groups agree on, where they diverge, what evidence supports each claim, and what decision should change.

## Recommended Positioning

Do not lead with:

- deliberative intelligence platform
- collective intelligence system
- AI deliberative democracy
- survey analysis tool
- Dovetail alternative

Lead with:

**Evidence-backed alignment for product and strategy decisions.**

Supporting line:

**UpSight shows where customers and stakeholders agree, where they disagree, which segments are driving each signal, and the source evidence behind every recommendation.**

This creates a commercial bridge:

1. Customer discovery wedge
2. Product and strategy alignment expansion
3. Organizational deliberation category
4. Civic/governance vertical later, after trust and proof exist

## Product Packaging Hypothesis

### Package 1: Product Alignment Pilot

For VP Product / Head of Research.

Outcome: turn 30-100 interviews, surveys, calls, or notes into an evidence-backed roadmap alignment brief.

Price: $15k-$30k for 8-10 weeks.

Deliverables:

- agreement and disagreement map
- segment-theme matrix
- top supported opportunities
- minority view report
- source evidence pack
- executive decision memo

### Package 2: Continuous Customer Evidence OS

For mid-market product and customer insights teams.

Outcome: continuously ingest customer conversations and survey responses into evidence-backed decision dashboards.

Price: $30k-$75k ARR.

### Package 3: Strategy / Advisory Board Intelligence

For exec staff, strategy, innovation, or advisory board programs.

Outcome: structure stakeholder input across recurring sessions into alignment, tradeoff, and recommendation maps.

Price: $50k-$150k ARR.

## Risks

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Category too abstract | Buyers may admire it but not buy | Sell decision pain, not category |
| Dovetail moves faster into customer intelligence | Strong brand and budget access | Differentiate on decision-grade alignment, conflict, provenance |
| Civic market is slow | Procurement can kill early velocity | Treat as later vertical or partner-led channel |
| Research teams lack budget authority | Loved by users, blocked by buyers | Sell to VP Product / CPO with research as champion |
| AI trust objection | Buyers fear hallucinated synthesis | Lead with evidence receipts, confidence, and audit trail |
| Too many use cases | Scattered GTM and weak proof | Start with one packaged pilot tied to a decision |

## Next Move

Run a 30-day market validation sprint around one wedge:

**Evidence-backed product alignment for high-stakes roadmap decisions.**

Target:

- 25 conversations with VP Product, Head of Research, Product Ops, and Customer Insights leaders
- 5 paid pilot proposals
- 2 signed paid design partners

Validation questions:

1. What high-stakes decision are they currently trying to make from messy customer/stakeholder input?
2. What tools do they use now?
3. Where does trust break down?
4. Who has to believe the synthesis for the decision to change?
5. What would they pay to turn the input into a credible decision memo in 2-4 weeks?

If buyers do not connect this to a decision with budget, the category is not ready. If they do, UpSight can credibly build from customer discovery into a broader deliberative intelligence platform.
