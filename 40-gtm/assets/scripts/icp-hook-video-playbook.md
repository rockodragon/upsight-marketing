# ICP Hook Video Playbook

> **What this is:** a repeatable recipe for the *first* short video you share to make one ICP want to talk — the "hook." Generalized from the Event Organizer hook (Riley/Outspoken) so any ICP can be done the same way.
> **Status:** v1 | **Date:** 2026-06-04 | **Owner:** rick
> **Inputs it pulls from:** [`30-strategy/icp/messaging-by-icp.md`](../../../30-strategy/icp/messaging-by-icp.md) · [`30-strategy/icp/README.md`](../../../30-strategy/icp/README.md) · brand voice in `30-strategy/brand-brief.md`
> **Worked example below:** Segment A — Founder-led B2B seller (the beachhead).

## 0. First principles (why this works)

- **Name it by its job: the Hook.** It's the top of a 3-rung ladder. Don't demo in it.
  1. **HOOK** (this) — make them *want* to talk · 45–75 sec
  2. **DEMO** — show how it works (e.g. the SmartSurveys script) · 2–3 min
  3. **PROOF** — a named case study with receipts · 60–90 sec
- **One ICP per video.** Specificity is the whole advantage. A generic UpSight video can't use their words; this one can.
- **One CTA.** A single, low-friction next step. Never "learn more."
- **Talking head + screen capture.** Your face for trust (open + invite); screen-recording for the capability + payoff.
- **~60 sec, captioned.** Most plays are muted. Burn in captions.

## The five rules we learned (apply to every ICP)

1. **Problem-first, not a competitor teardown.** Describe the *scramble* generically (spreadsheets, five tabs, chasing people) — don't name rivals. It dates the video and picks fights.
2. **Lead with the one capability competitors can't show.** For events it was the **AI survey kiosk**; for sellers it's **reads every call/email and tells you what to do**. Give that beat the most screen time — it's the "money shot."
3. **Anchor on the core value: all-in-one, tied to your data, with receipts.** Capture at the source → complete picture → insights *and* media/decisions, every one clickable back to the exact moment.
4. **Open in their words.** Borrow a real customer quote or the pain verbatim from `messaging-by-icp.md`. Real-operator language out-pulls founder language.
5. **Vision + invite, then stop.** A one-line "imagine if…" → the single CTA → tagline. End card with the book link.

## The reusable 5-beat skeleton

Each beat has a *function*. Fill the blanks per ICP.

| # | Beat | Function | Fill from |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Open — their truth** (0:00–0:12) | Earn the read. Quote their pain. | `messaging-by-icp` → "their pain (their words)" |
| 2 | **Agitate — the scramble** (0:12–0:30) | Make the status quo feel exhausting. Generic, no competitors. | their today's-tool / JTBD (README market map) |
| 3 | **Go to the source** (0:30–0:44) ★ | Show the one differentiated capability. | the capability that maps to this ICP's data |
| 4 | **The payoff — with receipts** (0:44–0:54) | All-in-one + tied to their data + decisions/media, citable. + drop a proof number. | core promise + "proof point" column |
| 5 | **Vision + invite** (0:54–1:06) | "Imagine if…" → ONE CTA → tagline. | "primary CTA" column |

### Fill-in-the-blanks template
```
[OPEN]      "<pain in their words>."  (Rick:) "Every <ICP> I talk to says some version of this."
[AGITATE]   "So you cobble it together — <their tools / the scramble>. And <the bad outcome>."
[SOURCE]    "Go right to the source. <the one capability>. So you build a complete picture of <who/why/how>."
[PAYOFF]    "It all lands in one place, tied to <their data>. <decisions/media> — every one with receipts.
             <Proof customer> got <proof number>, in an afternoon."
[INVITE]    "<imagine-if line>. I'm Rick — <CTA>." Tagline: "<headline reframed as a promise>."
```

### What to swap per ICP (everything else stays)
- **Open pain** + **proof point** + **CTA** → straight from the `messaging-by-icp` row.
- **Beat 3 capability** → the source-capture that fits their data (calls/email for sellers; kiosk/surveys for events; client calls for agencies).
- **Tagline** → reframe that ICP's one-line headline as a promise.
- **Distribution + hashtags** → that ICP's channels (README "Reach" column).

## Worked example — Segment A: Founder-led B2B seller (beachhead)

Pulled from `messaging-by-icp.md` row A. Pain: *"I'm losing deals I should win because I can't remember who said what."* Proof: *Cytodyme — 80 calls → 3 expansion accounts they'd missed, in an afternoon.* CTA: *"Send 10 calls, get the findings free."*

**SCENE 1 — OPEN: their truth (0:00–0:12)**
ON SCREEN: Founder at a laptop, late; a messy spreadsheet and a wall of Zoom recordings behind the lower-third.
VO (Rick, to camera): "If you run your own sales, you know this one — you lose a deal you should've won, because you couldn't remember who said what."

**SCENE 2 — AGITATE: the scramble (0:12–0:30)**
ON SCREEN: Spreadsheet rows, a list of un-watched recordings, sticky notes, a Slack search, five browser tabs.
VO: "Your calls are all there. In recordings you'll never rewatch, notes in five places, a spreadsheet that forgets. So you wing the follow-up — and the warm ones go cold."

**SCENE 3 — GO RIGHT TO THE SOURCE (0:30–0:44) ★**
ON SCREEN: Connect calls + email (or drop them in); UpSight transcribes/listens; contact profiles fill in automatically.
VO: "Better yet — go right to the source. Connect your calls and email, or just drop them in. UpSight reads every one and builds the picture for you — who you talked to, what they want, where each deal really stands."

**SCENE 4 — THE PAYOFF: decisions, with receipts (0:44–0:54)**
ON SCREEN: The Today view — Warm / Slipping / Blocked cards; click a card → the exact quote it's based on.
VO: "Then it tells you what matters: who's warm, who's slipping, what's blocking the next step. Every call backed by the exact quote — receipts, not guesses. Cytodyme ran 80 calls through it and found three expansion accounts they'd missed — in an afternoon."

**SCENE 5 — VISION + INVITE (0:54–1:06)**
ON SCREEN: Rick to camera / clean end card.
VO: "You closed the deals you could remember. Imagine closing the ones you forgot. I'm Rick — send me 10 of your calls and I'll send back the findings, free."
Tagline: "Know who's warm. Close what you'd have lost."
End card: UpSight logo · book/upload link.

### Distribution for Segment A (from README "Reach")
- **Build-in-public / founder channels first:** IndieHackers, MicroConf, YC networks, AI/founder Discords, Rick's own networks (StartupSD, Focus26, NCCC).
- **LinkedIn** native upload from personal profile; 1:1 DMs to founders doing their own sales.
- **Hashtags:** #buildinpublic #B2Bsales #founderledsales #startups #salestips
- **Money shot to feature:** the **Today view turning calls into Warm/Slipping/Blocked with one click to the quote** — the thing a spreadsheet or a notetaker can't do.

## How to produce it (mechanics)

1. Draft the 5 beats in a Google Doc (storyboard + teleprompter) — see the Event Organizer v3 doc for the format.
2. Optional designed deck: a `pptxgenjs` storyboard (navy/gold), one slide per beat + production/distribution/variant. Export to `.pptx`, drag into Drive for editable Slides.
3. Shoot talking-head for beats 1 & 5; screen-record beats 3 & 4. Caption. Keep ≤75 sec.
4. **Dogfood the loop:** collect reactions to the video *as video responses in UpSight* — feedback now, testimonials later.
5. A/B the opener (customer quote vs. the sharpest pain line). Log results in `40-gtm/experiments/_index.md`.

## Definition of done
- [ ] Opens with the ICP's pain in *their* words
- [ ] No competitor named; the scramble is the villain
- [ ] Beat 3 shows the one capability rivals can't
- [ ] Payoff = all-in-one + tied to their data + receipts, with one proof number
- [ ] Exactly one CTA, on the end card
- [ ] ≤75 sec, captioned, ICP-specific hashtags
