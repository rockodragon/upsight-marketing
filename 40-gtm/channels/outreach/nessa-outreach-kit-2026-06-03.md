## 1. Core message (the foundation)

**What UpSight IS (one sentence):**
UpSight is conversation intelligence plus a lightweight CRM that reads the calls, notes, and surveys you already have and tells you who's warm, who's slipping, and what's blocking the next step — with the exact quote behind every call.

**Who it's FOR (one sentence):**
Founder-led B2B sellers and small teams running their pipeline out of spreadsheets (or fighting a CRM that's too heavy) who are losing deals because the answer is buried in conversations nobody has time to re-listen to.

**What changes for them (one sentence):**
Instead of deciding who to chase by gut and memory, they open one screen that ranks who's ready to buy, who's going cold, and what's in the way — and they can click any claim to see who actually said it.

**One proof point (demonstrable, honest):**
Every insight links back to the verbatim quote and the person who said it — click a theme, see the receipts. No "the AI said so." (This is live and demoable today; we don't yet have a named-customer testimonial — see gaps note.)

**Phrases to use (from the vault):** *who's warm / who's slipping / what's blocking the next step* · *receipts* · *between sheets and silos* · *works on the data you already have* · *decisions, not dashboards* · *under $100/seat, no admin setup*.

**Phrases to avoid:** "AI-powered," "10x," "revolutionary"; leading with "research repository" or "customer intelligence platform"; "credits"; "CRM" first to spreadsheet users (say "upgrade from your spreadsheet").

## 2. Talk track (verbal — for calls)

### 30-second elevator
> "You know how when you're the one doing all the sales calls, the real answer to 'who's actually ready to buy' is buried in calls you'll never re-listen to? UpSight reads your calls, notes, and surveys and just tells you — who's warm, who's slipping, and what's blocking each deal. And every call it makes, you can click and see the exact quote behind it. It runs on the conversations you already have, so you see it on your own data in an afternoon."

### 2-minute version (opening a discovery call)
> "Quick context on why I reached out, then I mostly want to hear about you.
>
> Most founders and small teams I talk to are doing their own selling — discovery, demos, follow-up — and the information that would actually close a deal is sitting in calls, notes, and a spreadsheet that's three weeks stale. So decisions about who to chase end up running on memory and gut, and winnable deals go cold because nobody remembered to follow up.
>
> UpSight is the layer between the spreadsheet and the heavy tools like HubSpot or Gong. It reads the conversations you already have and hands you three things: who's warm right now, who's slipping, and what's blocking the next step on each one — each with the exact quote that flagged it, so you can trust it instead of taking the AI's word.
>
> The reason I wanted to talk: I'd rather show you on your own data than pitch you. If you send me a handful of recent call recordings or notes, I'll send back a one-page read on what's in them. But first — walk me through how you decide who to follow up with right now."

### Discovery questions (draw out the pain)
1. "Walk me through how you decide who to follow up with — what's that actually based on today?"
2. "Where do your customer conversations live right now — calls, notes, a spreadsheet, your head?"
3. "Last deal you lost that you feel like you should've won — what happened there?"
4. "When you say 'a customer told us X,' how do you back that up if someone on the team pushes back?"
5. "How much of what you learn on a call do you think you actually act on later?"

### The "land here" reframe (when they describe current pain)
- They say *"it's all in my head / a spreadsheet"* → "Right — and a spreadsheet remembers what was typed, but it can't tell you who's about to buy or about to disappear. That's the gap we sit in."
- They say *"we have notes / a CRM but never look at them"* → "That's the pattern. The data isn't the problem — nobody has time to mine it. UpSight does that part and just surfaces the decision."
- They say *"we run surveys / ask customers what they want"* → "Useful, but people tell you what they think they should say. The conversations are where what's actually true leaks out — that's what we read." *(Note: this "self-report vs. what's actually true" framing is from Rick's handoff; it's not yet a documented vault pillar — flagging so we use it consistently.)*

## 3. Cold email (written — built FROM the talk track)

- `the deal your spreadsheet forgot`

**Body:**
> Hi [First name],
>
> [Personalized opener — one line proving you looked. Pattern: reference something specific they do. e.g. *"Saw you're running [Company]'s sales yourself while still shipping product —"* or *"Noticed [Company] is still small enough that you're the one on every customer call —"*]
>
> …which usually means the answer to "who's actually ready to buy and who's about to go cold" is buried in calls and notes you'll never get time to re-listen to.
>
> Here's an offer instead of a pitch: **send me 5 of your recent call recordings or meeting notes, and I'll send back a one-page read** — who's warm, who's slipping, and what's blocking the next step on each, with the exact quote behind every call. Free, no meeting required.
>
> Worth a look at what's hiding in your own conversations?
>
> — [Name]

**CTA note:** the ask is *send 5 notes/calls*, not *book a meeting*. The free read earns the meeting. The deliverable has to be real — we actually run it and send back genuine findings.

## 4. Demo video script (60–90s, Rick on camera)

> Borrows the **Capture → Understand → Act** spine and the "receipts" beat from
> `upsight-demo-firm-v1.md`, restructured around the value-exchange offer. Rick on camera,
> screen-share cut-ins on one real (anonymized/consented) interview.

**[0–7s — Hook / the offer]**
> *(Rick, to camera)* "Send me five of your customer calls and I'll show you something you can't see right now. Let me show you what I mean — on a real one."

**[7–25s — The gap]**
> *(screen: a call recording / transcript)* "Here's a sales call. You took notes somewhere, you meant to follow up, and the thing that actually mattered is somewhere in 40 minutes you'll never replay. Multiply that by every call this month."

**[25–50s — Understand: the read + receipts]**
> *(screen: UpSight decision view)* "UpSight already read it. It's not a summary — it tells me this account is **warm**: here's the budget signal, here's the urgency. This one's **slipping** — went quiet after pricing came up. And it's **blocked** on one unanswered question. And watch this —" *(click)* "— every one of those links to the exact words they said. So I'm not trusting the AI. I'm reading my customer."

**[50–70s — Act: the missed thing]**
> "And here's the part you can't see in your notes: three different calls raised the same objection. You felt it, but you couldn't prove it. There it is, with all three quotes. That's the 'something you missed.'"

**[70–90s — CTA / the offer again]**
> *(back to camera)* "That took your calls and an afternoon — no setup, no migrating off your spreadsheet. So: send me five of your calls or notes. I'll send back who's warm, who's slipping, and what's blocking — on your data. [link / reply to this]."

## Handoff-vs-vault reconciliation notes (read before editing)

1. **The offer payload — sharpened on purpose.** Rick's handoff names the hook *"send us 5 notes,
   we'll show you something you missed."* But the **2026-06-02 revenue memo** explicitly says
   *"'what you missed' is directionally right but too vague... 'what is blocking the next step' is
   easier to prove and act on."* These assets keep the magic-trick *energy* (curiosity, "send 5,
   see what's hiding") but make the *deliverable* the concrete decision read. Rick: confirm, or
   pull back toward the vaguer version.

2. **Altitude — narrow, not the big category.** The handoff's "critical context" pushes a broad
   category story: *"customer-driven organization," the EOS wedge (vs Ninety.io / EOS One), the
   closed loop into a scorecard, "the operating layer that connects them."* **None of that phrasing
   exists in the vault** (grep: "customer-driven" = 0, "Ninety" = 0, "operating layer" = 0; "EOS"
   appears only as an internal product-surface name, not a GTM wedge). And the 6/2 memo deliberately
   chose the *opposite* — *"we will not lead with a broad customer-intelligence story... not
   competing on full CRM... not leading with research-repository language."* For **cold outreach**,
   narrow + concrete converts; the big category vision belongs on the website / deck / investor
   narrative. These assets stay narrow. Rick: confirm that's the right call for Nessa's outreach.
