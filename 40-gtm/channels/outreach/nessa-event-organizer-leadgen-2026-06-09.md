# Nessa Event-Organizer Lead-Gen — Research-as-Outreach

> Use *research* as the cold opener: a student/early-stage study on how event organizers
> handle attendee & sponsor feedback. The survey doubles as ICP research, a tagged prospect
> base in UpSight, and a dogfood of the survey / segmentation / rich-media features.
>
> **Frame it honestly as an ICP *probe* + dogfood, not "fill the funnel."** Leads are a
> byproduct. Success = learning whether event-organizer is a real ICP + a handful of
> qualified conversations + VOC verbatims — NOT raw lead volume.

---

## ⚠️ Strategic flag — resolve before launch

The canonical June-2026 positioning (`revenue-gtm-decision-memo-2026-06-02.md`) deliberately
**narrowed** to the **founder-led B2B seller running pipeline out of a spreadsheet**, and
explicitly chose *not* to lead with a broad customer-intelligence / research-repository story.

**Event organizers are a different persona with a different pain** (attendee feedback,
sponsor ROI) — not the warm/slipping/blocking *sales-decision* pain the product is built
around. So pick the reason, because it sets the budget:

- **(a) Real new-ICP bet** → needs product-fit validation, not just lead volume. The survey is ideal for this.
- **(b) Dogfood** UpSight's survey / segment / rich-media tooling on a live target → valuable regardless of leads.
- **(c) Keep intern productive + harvest VOC language** → fine, but time-box hard.

**Recommendation: run as (a)+(b), fixed time-box (~2 weeks of Nessa's time).** Tracked as an
open question (`00-control/open-questions.md`, 2026-06-09). If framed as "fill the funnel,"
off-ICP leads will *look* like progress and quietly pull GTM off the founder-led-seller thesis.

## Critique of the original idea

**Grades:** mechanic A− · ICP fit C · incentive design D · channel plan C+. All fixable.

### Strong
- Research framing lowers the ask + triggers the helping norm → 2–4× the reply rate of a pitch.
- SDSU-student angle is authentic and disarming. Use it.
- Double-duty: real ICP research *and* a tagged prospect base, one motion.
- Survey is well-built: screener → problem → signal, grounded in real stats, Q9 verbatims feed copy, Q10 is the pipeline filter.
- Rich-media replies → free VOC / testimonial content + a real feature dogfood.

### Broken (fix before launch)
1. **Muddled identity → RESOLVED: go with the edu angle.** Frame Nessa as an **SDSU marketing
   student** doing research, working with an early-stage team. Drop the "deeplight" name; UpSight
   is named only at the **handoff** step (Segment A), never in the cold opener. Honest, disarming,
   no bait-and-switch.
2. **$20 incentive is inverted** — dangling cash for "a call with a sr leader" makes the senior
   person the prize and the respondent the buyer (backwards). $20 attracts farmers, tanks data
   quality, and is too small to move a real senior organizer. **Fix:** drop cash; offer the
   **aggregated benchmark report back** — self-selects qualified/curious people, stronger B2B
   motivator. If cash, gift-card to *survey completers* (not call-takers) + attention check + ICP-email match.
3. **Group link-drops are the weakest channel — your own research says so** ("LinkedIn Groups
   Declared Dead," engagement low). Cold drops convert <1–2% and get flagged. Make group posting a
   *secondary awareness layer*, only where Nessa has first contributed.
4. **Don't put a sr leader on a $20 survey call.** Senior time = Segment A only, *after*
   qualification, as a *separate* follow-up. Nessa runs the research interviews herself.
5. **Survey too long for cold** — 10 Qs / 6 open-text ≈ 6–8 min; completion craters past ~3 min.
   **Split:** ≤4-question, mostly-closed, 90-sec screener survey → the *call* captures Q3–Q9.

## Fixed step-by-step plan

**Phase 0 — Decide & instrument (½ day)**
- Confirm scope = ICP-probe + dogfood, ~2-week time-box. Success = learning + N qualified convos, not lead count.
- UpSight: import Apollo list → build the **short** screener survey → create Segment A/B/C tags → enable rich-media replies.

**Phase 1 — Warm, don't cold (days 1–3)**
- 30 min/day: genuine comments on #eventprofs / #eventplanning posts + 2–3 target communities. Give before asking → makes Phase 2 DMs warm.

**Phase 2 — 1:1 research outreach (MAIN channel, days 2–10)**
- Personalized DM/email to 60–100 Apollo-matched organizers who are *actively posting*.
- Opener = the research line. Ask = 90-sec survey **or** 15-min chat. Incentive = benchmark report back.
- 3 touches max, stop on reply (per `sequences-by-icp` hygiene).

**Phase 3 — Group/community awareness layer (days 3–10)**
- Post the *student study* (not a survey-link drop) in 2–3 communities where Nessa contributed. Measure separately; expect underperformance vs 1:1.

**Phase 4 — Interviews & segment (rolling)**
- Nessa runs 15-min research calls — Q3–Q9 live here. Tag in UpSight:
  - **A** (4+ events/yr + yes-to-call) → hand to sr leader within 24h as a *separate* "want to see what we're building?" follow-up.
  - **B** (1–3/yr + maybe) → nurture / case-study email.
  - **C** (no) → mine Q9 verbatims for copy.

**Phase 5 — Close the loop (end of time-box)**
- Publish the aggregated benchmark (delivers the incentive promise + evergreen lead magnet).
- Write-up: *is event-organizer a real ICP?* → finding to `20-research/voice-of-customer/`; resolved call → `00-control/decisions.md` (closes the open question).

## Recommended survey structure (split)

**Cold screener (in-email/in-DM, ≤4 Qs, mostly closed, ~90 sec):**
- Q1 events/yr (MC) · Q2 event type (MC) · Q4-lite "happy with your post-event response rate?" (scale) · Q10 "open to a 15-min call?" (Yes/Maybe/No)

**Interview (the 15-min call captures the depth):**
- Original Q3, Q5, Q6, Q7, Q8, Q9 — these are interview questions, not cold-survey questions.
- Rich-media reply option offered to no-call respondents who still want to contribute (audio/video).

### B. Community-warm DM (after Nessa engages with their post)

> & sponsor feedback and demonstrate ROI. Your post on [topic] is right in the middle of it. Could I

## Expected success rate (funnel math, best-practice grounded)

Per **100 personalized, warmed research DMs:**

| Stage | Rate (research framing) | Count |
|---|---|---|
| Reply | 8–15% (vs 1–5% cold sales) | 8–15 |
| Agree to survey/interview | ~40–50% of repliers | 4–7 |
| Complete (short survey) | ~70% (long survey ~30–40%) | 3–5 |
| Yes-to-call → Segment A | 20–40% of completers | **1–3** |

- **~1–3 hot, ICP-qualified conversations per 100 personalized touches** — a normal *good* cold-TOF outcome.
- **Group link-drops:** budget <1–2% click, far fewer completes — bonus, not base case.
- **One batch of ~80–100 targeted contacts** ≈ 2–4 Segment-A convos + 5–8 nurture + a pile of VOC verbatims.
- Net: a respectable TOF *experiment*, **not** a scalable standalone channel.

## Alternatives, ranked (best leverage first)

1. **Association/chapter co-sign** (MPI, ILEA, PCMA, ASAE — see `events/gtm-conferences-communities.md`). A university student study is exactly what they amplify → credible distribution that beats cold group-drops ~10×. **Highest leverage.**
2. **Benchmark-report lead magnet** — "2026 Event Organizer Feedback Benchmark," gated, evergreen inbound. Turns a one-shot survey into a reusable asset.
3. **"Expert spotlight" interview** instead of a survey — higher-status ask, senior people say yes more, yields recorded content. Best for the hot tier.
4. **Live-community prospecting** — #eventhour Twitter chat, r/eventplanning, event-pro Slacks. Warmer than dead LinkedIn groups.
