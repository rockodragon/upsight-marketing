# Research-Led Lead-Gen — Founder-Led Seller / Spreadsheet SMB

> Parallel research-as-outreach track for the **primary ICP** — the founder-led B2B seller /
> small org running pipeline out of a spreadsheet (and fighting a CRM that's too heavy).
> Same mechanic as the event-organizer track, but **on-thesis**: this is exactly who the
> 6/2 revenue memo committed to. Research is the soft cold opener; the existing
> **"send me 5 of your calls, I'll send back a read"** offer (`nessa-outreach-kit`) is the
> conversion step. One funnel, two stages.

---

## How this fits the existing motion (read first)

The vault already has a strong **conversion** offer for this ICP — the value-exchange
"free read" in `nessa-outreach-kit-2026-06-03.md`. That offer is high-trust but a **big ask**
(send me your real calls). Many cold founders aren't there yet.

**Research is the lower-friction front door.** The funnel:

```
Research opener (this doc)  →  15-min "how do you run follow-up?" chat
        →  (if fit + engaged)  →  the free-read offer (nessa-outreach-kit)  →  trial
```

So this track does three jobs at once:
1. **Warms** cold founders toward the free-read offer they'd never accept cold.
2. **Validates the ICP pain in their words** → VOC for `20-research/voice-of-customer/` + Q9 verbatims for copy.
3. **Dogfoods** UpSight's survey / segmentation / rich-media tooling on the real target.

Unlike the event-organizer track, **no ICP flag** — this is on-thesis. Run it as a standing program, not a fixed-scope probe.

## Step-by-step plan

**Phase 0 — Seed + instrument (½ day)**
- Rick fills the **Seed research list** (below) — warm contacts first. Nessa starts here, not cold.
- UpSight: build the **short** screener survey → create Segment A/B/C tags → enable rich-media replies.

**Phase 1 — Warm seeds (days 1–5)**
- Nessa runs research chats with Rick's seed list (warm intros from StartupSD / Focus26 / NCCC / network).
- These give early VOC, testimonial language, referrals, and a credibility base ("I've talked to 12 founders who…").

**Phase 2 — Community-warm outreach (days 3–14, MAIN channel)**
- This ICP is **network/community-first** (per `sequences-by-icp` A + D), not cold-list-friendly.
- Nessa contributes genuinely in founder communities (StartupSD 1st Mondays, r/SaaS, r/startups, Lenny's/PLG Slacks), then DMs people who post about pipeline/follow-up/CRM pain.
- Opener = research line. Ask = 90-sec survey **or** 15-min chat. 3 touches max, stop on reply.

**Phase 3 — Run chats & segment (rolling)**
- Nessa runs 15-min research calls. Tag in UpSight:
  - **A** (founder-led + spreadsheet/abandoned-CRM + Yes-to-call) → within 24h, graduate to the **free-read offer**.
  - **B** (lightweight CRM + Maybe) → nurture / case-study email.
  - **C** (No) → mine Q9 verbatims for copy.

**Phase 4 — Close the loop (rolling)**
- VOC themes → `20-research/voice-of-customer/themes.md`. Sharpest verbatims → update outreach copy.
- Hot conversations → UpSight pipeline. Friction in the tool → `10-ops/dogfooding-log.md`.

## Outreach copy (research opener)

> Honest framing. Ask = a 15-min chat or the 90-sec survey. NO pitch in the opener — the
> chat earns the free-read offer later. Keep cold messages <90 words (per `sequences-by-icp` hygiene).

### A. Warm-seed intro (Rick → contact, or Nessa name-dropping Rick)
> Hey [name] — I'm working with Rick on UpSight, and we're doing a short study on how
> founder-led teams actually run sales follow-up — who you chase, what it's based on, where
> stuff slips. You run [company]'s sales yourself, so you're exactly who we want to learn from.
> 15 min, no pitch — just your honest workflow. I'll share back what we hear across everyone. Open to it?

### B. Community-warm DM (after Nessa has engaged with their post)
> Hi [name] — your post on [pipeline / chasing leads / dropping a CRM] is exactly what I'm
> researching: how small teams track customer relationships without a heavy CRM. I'm with an
> early-stage team (UpSight) studying this. Could I borrow 15 min — or 90 seconds for 4 questions?
> Not selling anything; I'll send back the aggregate findings + how your setup compares.

### C. Cold (only where firmographics fit — small B2B, no CRM in stack)
> Subject: how do you decide who to follow up with?
>
> Hi [name] — quick one. I'm researching how founders running their own sales decide who to
> chase when it's all in a spreadsheet and your head. If [company] is in that boat, would you
> answer 4 questions (90 sec) or do a 15-min chat? I'll share the aggregate back — no pitch.

### Follow-ups (2 touches max, stop on reply)
- **+3 days:** "No worries if buried — even the 4-question version helps (link). Happy to send the findings either way."
- **+5 days (breakup):** "Last one — I'll leave it open. If 'who's about to slip through the cracks' is ever a real question for you, the chat's a standing offer."

### The graduation line (Segment A, after the research chat)
> "This was really useful — thank you. One thing: what you described (the answer's buried in
> calls you'll never re-listen to) is the exact thing we built UpSight to fix. If you want, send
> me 5 recent calls or notes and I'll send back a one-page read — who's warm, who's slipping,
> what's blocking each — free, no meeting. Want me to run yours?"  *(→ hands to `nessa-outreach-kit`)*

## Short screener survey (90 sec, ≤5 Qs)

> Deep open-text questions move to the 15-min chat. The cold survey just qualifies + filters.

1. **How do you track your pipeline / customer relationships today?** (MC: Spreadsheet / Lightweight CRM / Heavy CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) / In my head / Other)
2. **Who does most of the selling and customer calls?** (MC: Me/founder / Small team / Dedicated sales)
3. **How confident are you that nothing's slipping through the cracks right now?** (Scale 1–5)
4. **Ever tried a CRM and abandoned it / found it too heavy?** (Yes / No / Currently fighting one)
5. **Open to a 15-min call on how you run follow-up?** (Yes / Maybe / No)

**Interview questions (the call):** where do call notes/recordings end up? · how do you decide who to follow up with? · last deal you should've won but lost — what happened? · how many tools touch one relationship? · *if you could instantly know one thing about your pipeline, what would it be?* (← Q9 verbatim gold).
Rich-media reply (audio/video) offered to no-call respondents who still want to contribute.

## Expected success rate (funnel math)

> 📊 **Visuals:** the full funnel set — the motion end-to-end, the cold *floor* (~1/100), the warm-seed
> *engine* (~1/20), and the report *flywheel* — lives in `nessa-run-sheet-2026-06-09.md` ("The funnels").

Per **100 community-warmed research touches** (warmer than pure cold → higher than the event-organizer track):

| Stage | Rate | Count |
|---|---|---|
| Reply | 12–20% (warm/community) | 12–20 |
| Agree to survey/chat | ~45–55% of repliers | 6–11 |
| Complete (short survey) | ~70% | 4–8 |
| Yes-to-call → Segment A | 25–45% of completers | **2–4** |
| Graduate to free-read offer | ~50% of Segment A | **1–2 trials-in-motion** |

- **Warm seeds convert far higher** — expect 40–60% of Rick's seed list to do a chat.
- Net per ~100 community touches: **2–4 hot founder convos + 1–2 free-read demos in motion + VOC.**
- This track's edge over the event-organizer one: it's **on-ICP**, so every conversation also de-risks the product thesis.
