# Day.ai head-to-head — can we win a seat at our own table?

- **Status:** planned
- **Opened:** 2026-06-02 | **Owner:** Rick
- **Source:** `30-strategy/revenue-gtm-decision-memo-2026-06-02.md` (Addendum §5), `20-research/market-intel/Competitors/dayai.md`

## Hypothesis
Founder-led SMBs evaluating Day.ai will choose UpSight when shown a **revenue-decision workflow on multi-source data (incl. surveys) with receipts, at a transparent price**, *for a defined sub-segment where Day.ai is weakest* — even though we won't match Day.ai on CRM automation.

## Why now
- You're already hearing the signal: **people balking at Day.ai's $75.** There's an umbrella below it.
- Day.ai **can't do surveys** and is call/email-centric → our multi-source + receipts is a concrete, demonstrable gap, not a claim.
- The AI-CRM category is wide open (Day.ai ~10 people, launched Feb 2026) → drafting is viable.

## Target sub-segment (niche down — "our own smaller table")
Pick the slice where our edge is *decisive*:
1. **Founder-sellers who also collect feedback/surveys** (Day.ai literally can't) — primary.
2. **Day.ai "$75 resisters"** — people who looked at Day.ai and walked on price.
3. (stretch) **Apollo refugees** who also evaluated Day.ai.

## What we do
1. Publish a **`upsight-vs-day.ai` comparison page** + an **"AI CRM that shows you who's warm, slipping, and blocked — and reads your surveys too, for a fraction of the price"** landing variant. (Draft the category per memo §1.)
2. **Concierge offer** (memo's offer): *"Send your calls, notes, Apollo export — and a survey if you have one — we'll show you who's warm, who's slipping, what's blocking the next step, with receipts."*
3. Targeted outreach to the sub-segment (LinkedIn + day.ai-curious communities + comparison-page retargeting).

## Variants (also test pricing)
- **A — value/outcome lead:** "who's warm / slipping / blocked + receipts + surveys" (no price headline).
- **B — draft/price lead:** "the Day.ai alternative that's transparent and reads everything" (price-forward).
- Optionally test the **packaging unit**: per-seat ($29/$39 live page) vs. **workspace + included usage** ($39 Starter) — which converts and at what willingness-to-pay?

## Primary metric
Qualified **demos booked → pilots committed** from the sub-segment, and **stated willingness-to-pay** at the workspace price.

## Guardrails / secondary
Comparison-page → signup rate; concierge-offer reply rate; % who cite surveys/multi-source as the deciding factor (validates the wedge).

## Decision rule
- **Win:** ≥2 pilots committed from ~10 targeted convos, and ≥1 cites surveys/multi-source as why → the lane + the workspace price are real; scale the comparison-page motion.
- **Kill/rethink:** 0–1 pilots, or they pick Day.ai on automation depth regardless → the wedge isn't decisive for this sub-segment; pick a different niche.
